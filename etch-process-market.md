<p><p>The etch process is pivotal in semiconductor manufacturing, enabling the precise patterning of materials. It is divided into two main types: dry etching and wet etching. Dry etching involves plasma or gaseous chemicals, typically offering better precision and control, making it suitable for intricate designs in advanced nodes. Wet etching uses liquid chemicals, providing a simpler, cost-effective approach for bulk material removal. Both markets are essential, catering to different applications in electronics, MEMS, and nanotechnology, each with unique advantages.</p></p>
